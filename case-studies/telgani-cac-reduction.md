# Case Study: CAC Reduction @ Telgani

**Role:** Senior Product Manager  
**Company:** Telgani  
**Domain:** Marketplace · Car Rental · MENA · Growth

---

## Context

Telgani is a car rental marketplace in Saudi Arabia and the broader GCC. I joined at a growth phase where the unit economics were under pressure — customer acquisition cost (CAC) had crept up as the company scaled paid channels, and the payback period was extending.

The mandate: improve CAC without compromising acquisition volume.

---

## Problem Definition

**Root cause analysis revealed three main drivers of high CAC:**

1. **Paid channel over-indexing:** 80%+ of new users came from paid search/social. No meaningful organic or referral flywheel.
2. **Low intent traffic:** Top-of-funnel targeting was too broad, bringing in users unlikely to convert in the near term.
3. **No retention engine:** Users who had rented once weren't being re-engaged systematically, forcing the team to re-acquire what should have been loyal customers.

---

## Approach

I ran a 4-week diagnostic across:
- Attribution data (channel-level CAC vs. LTV analysis)
- User cohort behavior (time-to-second-rental, churn patterns)
- Competitor positioning and channel mix analysis
- Interviews with sales/ops on what types of bookings were most profitable

Prioritized three workstreams: **channel mix rebalancing**, **targeting tightening**, and **retention loop**.

---

## Execution

**Channel Mix Rebalancing**
- Built a business case for investing in SEO content (travel guides, city-level car rental guides)
- Partnered with marketing to launch a referral program with symmetric incentives
- Result: organic share of new users increased from 12% to 22% over 2 quarters

**Targeting Precision**
- Worked with growth team to rebuild audience segments based on booking intent signals (search terms, session depth, return visit patterns)
- Eliminated broad-match campaigns that drove volume but not conversions
- Introduced bid adjustments based on predicted LTV by segment

**Retention Loop**
- Launched a "next rental" re-engagement sequence triggered 30 days post-rental
- Added loyalty tier visibility in-app to make progression tangible
- Built win-back campaign for users who hadn't rented in 90 days

---

## Outcome

| Metric | Result |
|---|---|
| Customer Acquisition Cost | ↓ 10% |
| Organic traffic share | ↑ from 12% to 22% |
| Repeat rental rate | ↑ 15% |
| Payback period | ↓ ~3 weeks |

---

## What I'd Do Differently

- Instrument LTV tracking earlier — we were making CAC decisions without a reliable LTV number for the first 6 weeks
- The retention loop should have launched before the targeting work; the economics of reactivating existing users are almost always better
