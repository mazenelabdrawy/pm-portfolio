# Case Study: PLG Growth Loop @ Squadio

**Role:** Senior Product Manager  
**Company:** Squadio  
**Domain:** SaaS · Product-Led Growth · B2B Marketplace

---

## Context

Squadio is a remote hiring platform connecting companies with vetted tech talent across MENA and beyond. The product had solid supply (engineers) but suffered from a leaky activation funnel on the demand side (companies hiring). Users were signing up but not reaching their first meaningful value moment.

---

## Problem Definition

**The core issue:** Users weren't activating because the path from signup to "I found a match I want to hire" was too long and ambiguous.

Activation was defined loosely across teams — some counted email verification, others counted posting a job. No single team owned the end-to-end new user experience.

**Discovery findings (from user interviews + funnel analysis):**
- 60%+ of signups never posted a job
- Of those who posted, 40% never reviewed a candidate
- The "first value moment" — receiving a shortlisted candidate — took 5–7 days on average
- Users churned before experiencing value

---

## Approach

I ran a 3-week discovery sprint:
- 12 user interviews with churned and retained customers
- Funnel analysis across 6 months of cohort data
- Benchmarked against similar PLG products (Notion, Loom, Typeform) to understand activation patterns

Redefined activation: **First shortlisted candidate reviewed within 48 hours of signup.**

Built a PLG activation roadmap focused on:
1. Reducing time-to-first-match
2. Progressive onboarding (not a wall of forms upfront)
3. Social proof injection at key drop-off points

---

## Execution

**Onboarding Redesign**
- Replaced multi-step requirements form with a 3-question intake ("role type, seniority, start timeline")
- Showed a preview of matching talent before job post completion
- Added contextual prompts showing "X companies like yours hired in 14 days"

**Activation Nudge System**
- Triggered emails + in-app nudges at 24h, 48h, 72h if users hadn't reviewed a candidate
- A/B tested subject lines and CTA framing (urgency vs. social proof)
- Built progress indicator showing users how close they were to their first match

**Conversion Optimization**
- Identified that users who completed a full job brief converted at 3x the rate of partial completions
- Added AI-assisted brief completion (suggested requirements based on role type)
- Removed friction from the "invite team member" step — made it optional, not a gate

---

## Outcome

| Metric | Result |
|---|---|
| Activation rate | ↑ 25% |
| Signup-to-first-candidate-reviewed | ↓ from 5.2 days to 2.8 days |
| Conversion (trial → paid) | ↑ 18% |
| Job brief completion rate | ↑ 34% |

---

## What I'd Do Differently

- Align on activation definition with leadership before starting — we lost 2 weeks debating the metric
- Run the A/B tests on the nudge copy earlier; it ended up being a bigger lever than the onboarding redesign
- Involve the CS team in discovery; they had the richest qualitative signal about why users churned
